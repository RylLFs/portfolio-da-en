Air Quality Classification Global AQI Using Data Mining

Classifying global air quality with decision tree

Context / Problem

In this fifth-semester project, I used Decision Tree Machine Learning in the RapidMiner application to classify air quality in several countries based on key pollutant parameters such as PM2.5, PM10, NO₂, SO₂, and O₃ against WHO standards. With a data-driven approach, this insight is expected to generate information about the Air Quality Index (AQI) that can be used for public education and more effective environmental policy decision-making.

Key Features & Insight

Global Air Quality

Comprehensive analysis of PM2.5, PM10, NO₂, SO₂, and O₃ with visualizations of their impact on AQI.

Decision Tree Model

Use of the C4.5 Decision Tree algorithm in RapidMiner with 99% accuracy.

WHO Standards

Classifying model weighting according to the established WHO standards.

Insights

Based on the analysis results, the data shows that air quality is dominated by poor quality, primarily due to PM2.5 with a ratio of 78.63%.
