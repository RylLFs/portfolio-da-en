Visualization and Analysis of IT Employee Salaries with Association Rules Algorithm

Analyzing salary correlation patterns using machine learning

Context / Problem

Academic Project for the third semester, I used the Apriori & Association Rules algorithm on a dataset of 400 IT professional profiles obtained from Kaggle. I identified strong patterns and correlations based on frequency and rules between position, years of experience, skill set, and salary range. These findings can help companies determine competitive compensation structures and assist IT professionals in strategically planning their career development.

Key Features & Insights

Apriori & Association Rules

Implementation of the Apriori & Association Rules algorithm on a dataset of 400 IT professionals with a minimum confidence of 70%.

Salary & Experience Categorization

Division of salary data into structured ranges (e.g., 40,000-120,000 USD) and Experience (e.g., Beginner (0-5 years)) for Association Rules encoding.

Career Factor Correlation Analysis

In-depth analysis of the relationship between job position, years of experience, technical skills, and salary range to identify the magnitude of IT employee salaries.

Insights

One of the insights obtained is that Entry-level Web Developers (0-5 years) have a salary range of $40K-$120K.
